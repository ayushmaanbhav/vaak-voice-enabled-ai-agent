# India Gold Loan Market: Comprehensive Research Report (2024-2025)

## Executive Summary

The India gold loan market is experiencing unprecedented growth, valued at approximately **₹7.1 lakh crore (USD 67.40-83.69 billion)** in 2024, with projections to reach **₹14.19 lakh crore (USD 157.60-243.77 billion)** by 2028-2031. The market has grown at a **14.85% CAGR** over the last five years, with **71.3% year-over-year growth** in bank gold loan disbursements in the first nine months of FY25.

### Loan-to-Value (LTV) Ratio

- **Standard LTV:** **70-75%** (lenders offer loans against large portion of gold's worth)
- **RBI Ceiling:** Maximum **75%** for retail gold loans (pre-2025)
- **New RBI Framework (2025):**
  - Up to **₹2.5 lakh:** **85% LTV**
  - **₹2.5 lakh - ₹5 lakh:** **80% LTV**
  - Above **₹5 lakh:** **75% LTV**
- **NBFC Average Portfolio LTV:** **60-65%** on mark-to-market basis (June 2024), providing adequate cushion against gold price fluctuations
- **Muthoot & Manappuram:** **63.4%** and **60.3%** respectively in Q1 FY25 (much below the 75% cap)

## 7. RBI Regulations & Compliance

### September 2024 Circular (RBI/2024-25/77)

**Date:** September 30, 2024
**Addressed to:** All commercial banks, primary (urban) co-operative banks, and NBFCs undertaking lending against pledge of gold/jewellery

**Deadline for Compliance:** December 30, 2024 (3 months)

### Key Irregularities Identified

| Violation | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Third-Party Issues** | Shortcomings in use of third parties for sourcing and appraisal |
| **Valuation Without Customer** | Gold valued without customer presence |
| **Inadequate Due Diligence** | Lack of end-use monitoring of gold loans |
| **Auction Transparency** | Lack of transparency during auction of gold on default |
| **LTV Monitoring Weaknesses** | No robust system for periodic LTV monitoring; breaches in regulatory LTV ceilings; system alerts not followed up |
| **Incorrect Risk Weights** | Misapplication of risk-weights |
| **Top-up Loans** | Issued without fresh appraisals |
| **Evergreening** | Overdue loans renewed/replaced without addressing repayment concerns |
| **Cash Disbursement** | Exceeded statutory limits under Income Tax Act; high proportion in cash |

### New LTV Framework (2025)

| Loan Amount | LTV Cap |
|-------------|---------|
| Up to ₹2.5 lakh | 85% |
| ₹2.5 lakh - ₹5 lakh | 80% |
| Above ₹5 lakh | 75% |

### IIFL Finance Crisis (March 2024)

**RBI Action:** Barred from sanctioning/disbursing gold loans on March 4, 2024

**Violations Found:**
- Deviations in assaying and certifying purity and net weight of gold
- Breaches in LTV ratio
- Significant cash disbursements/collections far exceeding statutory limit
- Non-adherence to standard auction process
- Lack of transparency in charges

**Resolution:** Restrictions lifted in September 2024
