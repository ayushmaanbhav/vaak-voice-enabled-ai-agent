## Executive Summary

The India gold loan market is experiencing unprecedented growth, valued at approximately **₹7.1 lakh crore (USD 67-84 billion)** in 2024, with projections to reach **₹14.19 lakh crore** by 2028. The market demonstrates a **14.85% CAGR** over the last five years, with bank gold loan disbursements surging **71.3% YoY** in FY25.

### Key Market Highlights

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Market Size (2024)** | ₹7.1 lakh crore |
| **Projected Size (2028)** | ₹14.19 lakh crore |
| **5-Year CAGR** | 14.85% |
| **Unorganized Sector Share** | 63-65% |
| **NBFC Market Share (Organized)** | 61-63% |
| **Bank Market Share (Organized)** | 31-37% |
| **Gold Monetization Rate** | Only 5.6% of household gold |
| **Total Household Gold** | 25,000-27,000 tonnes |

### Critical Findings

1. **Massive Untapped Potential:** Only 5.6% of India's household gold (approximately 25,000-27,000 tonnes worth ₹126 lakh crore) has been monetized through loans

2. **South India Dominance:** The southern region accounts for **80%** of outstanding gold loans, led by Kerala (18% of household debt is gold loans)

3. **Women Driving Growth:** Women's share in gold loans grew **6%** since 2019, with **35%** of business borrowers being women by December 2024

4. **MSME Surge:** Gold loan disbursals for MSMEs increased from **12% to over 30%** in the last three years, with 75% of Muthoot Finance customers being MSMEs

5. **Digital Transformation:** Online gold loan searches increased **57.47%** in 2024; digital adoption accelerated significantly post-COVID

6. **Competitive Landscape:** Muthoot Finance (38%), IIFL (13%), and Manappuram (12%) dominate the NBFC segment; banks are aggressively entering with lower rates

### 5.4 Failure Analysis

- Banned from sanctioning/disbursing gold loans
- Violations: LTV breaches, cash disbursement limits, auction process non-adherence
- Restrictions lifted: September 2024

#### Key Irregularities Identified by RBI

| Violation | Issue |
|-----------|-------|
| **Third-Party Valuation** | Without customer presence |
| **Inadequate KYC** | Especially via fintech partnerships |
| **Loan Usage Tracking** | No verification of fund usage |
| **Evergreening** | Principal never repaid, loans perpetually renewed |
| **Unfair Auctions** | Lack of transparent price discovery |

#### Rising NPAs

- **December 2023:** ₹5,307 crore

- **Growth:** ₹1,500+ crore increase

2. **Custody Worries:** Anxiety about gold safety during transport
3. **Data Privacy:** Concerns about personal data security
4. **Digital Literacy:** Limited in Tier 2/3 cities and rural areas
5. **Regulatory Uncertainty:** Digital gold outside SEBI/RBI purview

### 6.5 Key Lessons Learned

#### What Worked

1. **Digital is essential** - COVID accelerated years of transformation
2. **Trust is paramount** - Transparency in pricing and process critical
3. **Hybrid approach works** - Customers value human touch for gold
4. **Speed matters** - Sub-30-minute disbursement became standard
5. **Flexibility is key** - Multiple repayment options reduce defaults

#### Challenges Faced

1. **Gold Price Volatility:** Manappuram auctions rose from Rs 8 Cr to Rs 404 Cr in one quarter
2. **Technology Scalability:** Initial apps had volume limitations
3. **Collection Challenges:** Bounce rates surged from 19% to 86% during lockdown
4. **Customer Education Gap:** Many didn't understand moratorium implications
5. **Marketing vs. Profitability:** Manappuram PAT dropped 44.3% despite 300% marketing increase

## Part 7: Regulatory Framework

### 7.1 RBI Guidelines 2024-2025

#### September 2024 Circular (RBI/2024-25/77)

**Key Issues Addressed:**
- Third-party sourcing and appraisal shortcomings
- Gold valued without customer presence
- Inadequate end-use monitoring
- Lack of auction transparency
- LTV monitoring weaknesses
- Incorrect risk weights
- Top-up loans without fresh appraisals
- Evergreening of loans
- Cash disbursement exceeding statutory limits

**Compliance Deadline:** December 30, 2024 (3 months)

#### New LTV Framework (2025)

| Loan Amount | LTV Cap |
|-------------|---------|
| Up to ₹2.5 lakh | 85% |
| ₹2.5 lakh - ₹5 lakh | 80% |
| Above ₹5 lakh | 75% |

#### Bullet Repayment Restrictions

- Renewals/top-ups only after **full interest repayment**
- Trend shift to shorter tenor products (3-6 month maturities)
- Reduced flexibility for bullet loans

### 7.2 Compliance Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Cash Disbursal Limit** | ₹20,000 (May 2024 RBI directive) |
| **KYC** | Continuous monitoring; Aadhaar, PAN validation |
| **Gold Custody** | Appropriate storage facilities required |
| **Insurance** | Full value coverage mandatory |
| **Auction Process** | Transparent with adequate notice to borrowers |
| **Documentation** | More stringent requirements |

### 7.3 Recent Enforcement Actions

#### IIFL Finance Crisis (March 2024)

**Violations Found:**
- Deviations in assaying and certifying purity
- Breaches in LTV ratio
- Significant cash disbursements exceeding limits
- Non-adherence to standard auction process
- Lack of transparency in charges

**Resolution:** Restrictions lifted September 2024
