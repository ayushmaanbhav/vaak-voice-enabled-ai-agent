# GOLD LOAN FAILURES, BLUNDERS, AND COMPLIANCE VIOLATIONS IN INDIA
## Comprehensive Research Report on RBI Enforcement Actions, Scandals, and Operational Failures

**Research Date:** December 22, 2025
**Prepared for:** Kotak Mahindra Bank - Gold Loan Strategy Study

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report documents major failures, compliance violations, fraud cases, and operational issues in India's gold loan industry from 2020-2025. The research reveals systematic regulatory violations by major NBFCs, internal fraud by employees, auction irregularities, and significant RBI enforcement actions that have shaped the industry landscape.

**Key Findings:**
- IIFL Finance faced complete ban on gold loan disbursement (March 2024)
- Four NBFCs barred from lending in October 2024 for regulatory violations
- Rising NPAs: Rs 4,470 crore for NBFCs and Rs 2,162 crore for banks (March 2025)
- Multiple employee fraud cases involving fake gold and loan evergreening
- Industry-wide issues with LTV breaches, cash disbursement violations, and auction transparency

## 1. RBI ENFORCEMENT ACTIONS AND PENALTIES

### 1.1 IIFL Finance - Complete Gold Loan Ban (March 2024)

**Date:** March 4, 2024
**Action:** Complete prohibition on sanctioning and disbursing gold loans
**Portfolio Impact:** Rs 24,692 crore (32% of total loans)

**Violations Identified:**
1. **Gold Assaying Deviations:** Serious deviations in assaying and certifying purity and net weight of gold at the time of loan sanction and auction upon default
2. **LTV Ratio Breaches:** Violations of loan-to-value ratio limits (regulatory ceiling of 75%)
3. **Excessive Cash Transactions:** Significant disbursement and collection of loan amounts in cash far exceeding the statutory limit of Rs 20,000
4. **Auction Process Violations:** Non-adherence to standard auction procedures and lack of transparency in customer charges
5. **No Corrective Action:** Despite RBI engagement with senior management and statutory auditors, no meaningful corrective action was evidenced

**Quote from RBI:** "These practices, apart from being regulatory violations, also significantly and adversely impact the interest of the customers."

**Resolution:** RBI lifted restrictions in September 2024 after a special audit and compliance improvements. The ban lasted approximately 6 months.

**Source:** Reserve Bank of India Press Release, March 4, 2024

### 1.2 Four NBFCs Barred from Lending (October 2024)

**Date:** October 17, 2024

### 1.3 Industry-Wide Gold Loan Warning (September-October 2024)

**Date:** September 30, 2024
**RBI Circular:** "Gold loans - Irregular practices observed in grant of loans against pledge of gold ornaments and jewellery"
**Compliance Deadline:** December 30, 2024 (3 months)

**Major Deficiencies Identified:**

**A. Third-Party Service Issues:**
- Improper use of fintech entities and Business Correspondents for loan sourcing and appraisal
- Gold valuation conducted without customer presence
- Credit appraisal and valuation handled by BCs
- Gold stored in BC custody instead of bank branches
- Delayed and insecure transportation of gold to branches
- KYC compliance managed through Fintechs
- Use of internal accounts for disbursement and repayment

**B. Auction Irregularities:**
- Lack of transparency during auction of gold on customer default
- Customers received lower realization from auctions than estimated gold value
- Average auction proceeds below market value, indicating valuation gaps

**C. LTV Monitoring Failures:**
- Lack of robust systems for periodic LTV monitoring
- System-generated alerts not pursued
- Breaches in regulatory LTV ceilings not addressed

**D. Loan Evergreening:**
- No specific identifier for top-up gold loans in core banking systems
- Practice designed to facilitate evergreening of loans
- No fresh appraisal conducted for top-up loans
- Loans renewed at tenor end with only partial payment
- Non-categorization of NPAs to mask bad loans

**E. Cash Disbursement Violations:**
- High proportion of gold loans disbursed in cash
- Statutory limit of Rs 20,000 under Income Tax Act, 1961 frequently violated
- Incorrect application of risk weights

**F. Due Diligence Gaps:**
- Inadequate due diligence on borrowers
- Lack of end-use monitoring for loan funds
- Improper document retention for agriculture gold loans

**RBI Action:** Directed all supervised entities to review policies within 3 months and report to Senior Supervisory Manager. Non-compliance would attract supervisory action.

## 3. OPERATIONAL FAILURES AND SYSTEMIC ISSUES

### 3.1 Gold Theft and Security Incidents

While specific large-scale theft incidents at major NBFC branches are not extensively documented in recent news, the Manappuram Sambalpur case (January 2025) highlighted critical security vulnerabilities:

**Key Security Concerns:**
- Branches storing Rs 20+ crore worth of gold with zero security personnel
- Inadequate vault security despite insurance requirements
- Delayed and insecure transportation of gold from BC/fintech locations to branches
- Gold stored in Business Correspondent custody instead of secure bank vaults

**Regulatory Requirements (Often Violated):**
- Ample systems for keeping valuables in secure possession
- Ongoing system reviews and audits
- Employee training on security protocols
- Periodic internal auditor inspections
- Vaults/safes meeting insurance and security standards
- Proper insurance of accepted gold collateral

---

### 3.2 Auction Irregularities and Customer Losses

**RBI Findings (September 2024 Circular):**

**A. Low Auction Realization:**
- Average realization from gold auctions lower than estimated gold value
- Customers suffering financial losses due to undervaluation
- Gaps in valuation processes leading to reduced auction proceeds

**B. Lack of Transparency:**
- Opaque auction processes
- Customers not adequately informed about auction procedures
- Insufficient public notification (violations of 2-newspaper requirement)
- No arm's length relationship maintained in transactions

**C. Procedural Violations:**
- Inadequate prior notice to borrowers before auction
- Non-transparent charging structures
- Auctions not conducted through board-approved auctioneers
- NBFCs potentially participating in their own auctions (conflict of interest)
- Excess realization not refunded within mandated 30 days

**D. Legal Framework Violations:**

**Required Auction Procedures (Often Not Followed):**
1. Transparent process as per laid-down procedures
2. Adequate prior notice to borrower
3. No conflict of interest
4. Arm's length relationship in all transactions
5. Public announcement in at least 2 newspapers (1 vernacular, 1 national)
6. Only board-approved auctioneers
7. NBFC cannot participate in auction
8. Excess proceeds refunded within 30 days
9. Option to redeem ornaments till previous working day before auction

**Customer Legal Remedies:**
- Consumer Disputes Redressal Commissions
- High Courts under Article 226 for fundamental rights violations
- RBI Integrated Ombudsman Scheme
- Civil suits for recovery or damages
- Criminal prosecution for wrongful auction

**Landmark Court Positions:**
- Courts have held lenders liable for compensation for wrongful auctions
- Unconscionably high interest rates struck down as against public policy
- Criminal prosecution directed for gold tampering cases

## 4. EVERGREENING AND NPA CONCEALMENT PRACTICES

### 4.1 Systematic Evergreening Methods

**Common Practices Identified by RBI:**

**A. Roll-Over Schemes:**
- Loans renewed at end of tenor with only partial interest payment
- Principal not repaid, just extended
- New loan sanctioned to pay off overdue loan
- Practice disguises actual NPAs

**B. Top-Up Loan Abuse:**
- No specific identifier in core banking systems
- Designed to facilitate evergreening
- No fresh appraisal conducted
- Borrower adds small amount to existing loan to reset tenure

**C. System Manipulation:**
- Non-categorization of 90+ day overdue accounts as NPAs
- Inadequate monitoring by senior management/board
- Absence of controls over third-party entities
- System alerts generated but not pursued

**D. Loan Renewal Near Repayment Date:**
- Requests made just before due date
- Strategy to avoid NPA classification
- Misleading financial reporting
- Inflated loan disbursement numbers

**E. Netting Off Practices:**
- Under RBI scrutiny for hiding borrower stress
- Disguising true collection efficiency
- Masking systemic asset downgrades

---

### 4.2 Regulatory Countermeasures

**RBI Actions to Combat Evergreening:**

**September 2023 Intervention:**
- Previously: Borrowers could repledge jewellery by paying only interest
- Post-intervention: Repledging requires full repayment of principal and interest
- Impact: Made loan extensions costlier, limiting evergreening

**April 2025 Draft Guidelines:**
- Gold loans can only be renewed/topped up if still "standard loans"
- Must follow LTV limits
- Fresh appraisals mandatory for top-ups
- Specific identifiers required in systems

**Ongoing Monitoring:**
- Banks devised systems to track evergreening
- Offsite tracking to detect loans closed soon after sanctioning
- Enhanced supervisory oversight
- Mandatory reporting to RBI within 3 months

## 5. SYSTEMIC COMPLIANCE ISSUES

### 5.1 Loan-to-Value (LTV) Ratio Breaches

**Regulatory Requirement:** Maximum 75% LTV for gold loans

**Common Violations:**
- Loans exceeding 75% of gold value at sanctioning
- No robust system for periodic LTV monitoring
- System alerts generated but not acted upon
- Valuation errors at time of lending
- Gold price appreciation not monitored during loan tenure
- Re-appraisal not conducted for long-tenure loans

**Consequence:** When gold prices fall or loans accumulate interest, actual LTV exceeds regulatory limits, creating risk

---

### 5.2 Cash Disbursement Violations

**Regulatory Limit:** Rs 20,000 per loan under Income Tax Act, 1961

**Violations:**
- IIFL Finance: "Significant disbursements in cash far in excess of statutory limit"
- High proportion of gold loans disbursed entirely in cash
- Used to avoid digital trail and tax reporting
- RBI warned NBFCs specifically in May 2024

**Implications:**
- Tax evasion facilitation
- Money laundering risks
- Lack of transparency
- Difficulty in tracking fund flows

---

### 5.3 Know Your Customer (KYC) Failures

**Common KYC Violations:**

**A. Multiple UCIC Assignments:**
- Same customer given multiple unique identification codes
- Defeats purpose of centralized customer identification
- Muthoot Finance penalized Rs 1.9 lakh for this

**B. PAN Card Non-Compliance:**
- Failure to obtain PAN copies for loans >Rs 5 lakh
- Muthoot Finance penalized Rs 10 lakh (2020)

**C. Outsourcing KYC to Third Parties:**
- HDFC Bank penalized for outsourcing KYC verification to external agents
- Prohibited under RBI norms
- Creates risk of inadequate verification

**D. Third-Party KYC Through Fintechs:**
- Industry-wide issue flagged by RBI (September 2024)
- KYC compliance managed through fintech partners
- Lack of direct bank oversight

---

### 5.4 Risk Weight Misapplication

**Issue:** Incorrect application of risk weights to gold loan portfolios

**Impact:**
- Under-provisioning for potential losses
- Capital adequacy ratios artificially inflated
- Systemic risk underestimation

**RBI Finding:** Entities did not follow regulations while applying risk weights for gold loans

---

### 5.5 Liquidity Risk Management Failures

**Muthoot Vehicle & Asset Finance Case (2024):**
- Did not disclose Liquidity Coverage Ratio on website
- Penalty: Rs 7.9 lakh
- Violation of RBI's Liquidity Risk Management Framework for NBFCs

**Industry-Wide Concerns:**
- Rapid portfolio growth without commensurate liquidity planning
- Over-reliance on short-term funding for long-term assets
- Inadequate stress testing

## 7. REGULATORY EVOLUTION AND TIGHTENING

### 7.1 New Gold Loan Rules (Proposed April 2025, Effective January 2026)

**Key Provisions:**

**A. Return of Gold Post-Repayment:**
- Gold must be returned within 7 working days of full loan repayment
- Lender penalty: Rs 5,000 per day for delay beyond 7 days
- Significant improvement in customer protection

**B. LTV Limits:**
- Maximum 75% LTV maintained
- Ban on loans against gold bars (only jewellery/ornaments)
- Cap on pledge limits

**C. Renewal and Top-Up Restrictions:**
- Only allowed for "standard loans" (not NPAs)
- Must comply with LTV limits
- Fresh appraisal mandatory

**D. Political Intervention:**
- Tamil Nadu CM MK Stalin raised concerns
- Finance Ministry suggested exemption for loans below Rs 2 lakh
- Balances consumer protection with accessibility

**E. Enhanced Supervision:**
- Violations may lead to supervisory action
- Penalties up to Rs 1 crore
- Potential license revocation under Section 47A of RBI Act

---

### 7.2 Fraud Risk Management Directions (2024)

**RBI Notification:** Reserve Bank of India (Fraud Risk Management in NBFC) Directions, 2024

**Key Requirements:**

**A. Fraud Reporting:**
- Report fraud incidents to RBI through Fraud Monitoring Returns (FMR) within 14 days
- Theft, burglary, robbery: Report within 7 days
- Quarterly returns for such incidents

**B. Fraud Categories Covered:**
- Forgery
- Misappropriation of funds
- Manipulation of books of accounts
- Employee fraud
- Customer fraud
- Cyber fraud

**C. Prevention Framework:**
- Early detection systems
- Robust internal controls
- Employee training
- Periodic audits

---

### 7.3 RBI's Escalating Enforcement Approach

**Governor Das Statement:**
"Wherever we see major deviations in compliance and regulatory requirements, our first intent is to deal directly, bilaterally with them, sensitize them, work with them, and impress upon them to take corrective action. While working with them, when progress is not up to the mark, then on top of that, we apply supervisory restrictions."

**Enforcement Pattern:**
1. **Engagement:** Direct dialogue with management and auditors
2. **Warning:** Circulars and notices
3. **Restrictions:** Product-specific bans (IIFL case)
4. **Penalties:** Monetary fines (various amounts)
5. **License Action:** Suspension or revocation (rare but possible)

**Recent Trend:** Zero tolerance for persistent non-compliance

**2023-2024 Actions:**
- Risk weight hikes for unsecured loans (November 2023)
- IIFL Finance gold loan ban (March 2024)
- Four NBFCs lending ban (October 2024)
- Industry-wide circular on gold loans (September 2024)
- Multiple penalties on Muthoot and Manappuram entities

## 8. IMPLICATIONS FOR KOTAK MAHINDRA BANK STRATEGY

### 8.1 Competitive Advantages from Competitor Failures

**Market Opportunity:**
- Customer trust eroded in major NBFCs due to scandals
- Gold safety concerns create opening for bank-based offerings
- Regulatory compliance as differentiator
- Bank reputation advantage over NBFCs

**Key Differentiators:**
1. **Security:** Bank vaults vs NBFC branches with inadequate security
2. **Transparency:** Clear auction processes with proper documentation
3. **Compliance:** No history of RBI penalties in gold loan segment
4. **Digital Safety:** Robust IT controls vs NBFC vulnerabilities
5. **Grievance Redressal:** Established bank ombudsman mechanisms

---

### 8.2 Risks to Avoid

**Critical Compliance Areas:**

**A. LTV Management:**
- Implement robust real-time LTV monitoring
- Automated alerts with mandatory follow-up protocols
- Periodic revaluation triggers
- Clear escalation matrix for LTV breaches

**B. Cash Disbursement Controls:**
- Strict adherence to Rs 20,000 cash limit
- Digital-first disbursement approach
- Audit trails for all cash transactions

## 9. CONCLUSION AND STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS

### 9.1 Key Takeaways

**Industry Landscape:**
1. Gold loan NBFC sector under intense RBI scrutiny (2023-2025)
2. Systematic compliance failures across major players
3. Rising NPAs: Rs 6,632 crore industry-wide (March 2025)
4. Customer trust eroded by employee fraud and operational failures
5. Regulatory framework tightening significantly

**Major Failure Categories:**
- **Regulatory Violations:** LTV breaches, cash disbursement, KYC failures
- **Operational Failures:** Security lapses, auction irregularities, grievance handling
- **Fraud:** Employee collusion, loan evergreening, fake gold, system manipulation
- **Governance:** Inadequate board oversight, poor internal controls, fintech risks

- **Highest:** IIFL Finance - Complete ban on gold loan business (6 months)
- **Widespread:** Four NBFCs barred simultaneously (October 2024)
