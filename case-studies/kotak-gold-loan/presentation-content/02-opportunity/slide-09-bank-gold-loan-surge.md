## Speaker Notes

### Opening - Momentum Statement (10 seconds)
"Something extraordinary is happening in the gold loan market. Banks are growing at 71.3% year-on-year - the highest growth rate ever recorded for this segment."

### The Shift (25 seconds)
"Let me put this in perspective. NBFCs have dominated gold loans for decades - they still hold 83% of the market. But they're growing at just 8.2%. Banks are growing almost 9 times faster.

At this rate, banks will control a third of the gold loan market by 2028. That's a complete restructuring of a 7-lakh-crore industry happening right now, in real-time."

### Why This Is Happening (30 seconds)
"Three forces are driving this shift. First, rate advantage. We can offer 9-12% against NBFC rates of 18-27%. That's a 40-50% savings for customers - on a 5-lakh loan, that's 70,000 rupees saved.

Second, trust. After the IIFL ban and Muthoot penalties, 67% of customers now have concerns about NBFC gold safety. They're asking: 'Is my gold secure?' Banks win that trust battle.

Third, regulatory compliance. The September 2024 RBI circular tightened gold loan norms. Banks were already compliant - NBFCs are scrambling to catch up, adding 15-20% to their operating costs."

### Competitive Position (20 seconds)
"Now here's where it gets interesting for us. Federal Bank grew 84%. HDFC grew 72%. The average bank grew 71%. Kotak? We grew 42%.

We're growing, yes - but we're leaving money on the table. At peer growth rates, we'd have an 8,800-crore gold loan book instead of 4,800 crores. That's 500 crores in lost revenue this year alone."

### The Urgency (15 seconds)
"This window won't stay open forever. We're at the inflection point - right when customer trust in NBFCs is shaking, before they drop their rates to compete, before the market saturates with bank offerings.

The next 18 months will determine if we capture significant market share or watch our competitors do it."

### Transition (5 seconds)
"So we have momentum, we have market shift. The question is: why RIGHT NOW specifically? Let's look at the three forces converging..."
